## User Personas

### Persona 1: Adaeze (The Tenant)

**Demographics:**
- Age: 28
- Occupation: Junior accountant
- Location: Lagos
- Tech savviness: Moderate (uses WhatsApp, Instagram daily)
- Income: ₦150,000/month

**Goals:**
- Understand her rights as a tenant
- Know what her landlord can and cannot do
- Avoid being cheated or exploited

**Pain Points:**
- Landlord is demanding rent increase mid-lease
- Doesn't know if this is legal
- Can't afford a lawyer for consultation
- Online search returns confusing legal jargon

**Behaviors:**
- Searches "tenant rights Nigeria" on Google
- Asks in WhatsApp groups for advice (unreliable)
- Avoids confrontation due to uncertainty

**Needs from LawMadeSimple:**
- Plain language explanation of tenancy law
- Practical example: "Can my landlord increase rent before lease ends?"
- Clear answer with source citation
- Guidance on what to do next

**Quote:** *"I just want to know if what my landlord is doing is legal or not."*

---

### Persona 2: Chukwuemeka (The SME Owner)

**Demographics:**
- Age: 35
- Occupation: Owns a small logistics company
- Location: Abuja
- Tech savviness: High (uses business software, online banking)
- Income: ₦500,000/month

**Goals:**
- Register his business properly under CAMA
- Understand tax obligations (FIRS)
- Ensure employment contracts are compliant

**Pain Points:**
- Overwhelmed by legal requirements for business
- Received contradictory advice from different sources
- Worried about getting in trouble with authorities
- Lawyers are expensive for routine compliance questions

**Behaviors:**
- Searches for business registration guides
- Reads multiple articles, still confused
- Pays for sporadic lawyer consultations
- Delays compliance due to confusion

**Needs from LawMadeSimple:**
- Step-by-step business registration guidance
- Tax obligation explanations with examples
- Employment law basics for hiring staff
- SME-specific content section

**Quote:** *"I want to do things the right way, but I don't know what the right way is."*

---

### Persona 3: Tunde (The Employee)

**Demographics:**
- Age: 24
- Occupation: Sales associate at retail company
- Location: Port Harcourt
- Tech savviness: High (Gen Z, very online)
- Income: ₦80,000/month

**Goals:**
- Know his rights as an employee
- Understand if his employer is treating him fairly
- Know what to do if wrongfully terminated

**Pain Points:**
- Working long hours without overtime pay
- Employer threatens termination frequently
- No written contract despite working for 8 months
- Feels powerless against employer

**Behaviors:**
- Complains on Twitter/X about work conditions
- Searches "labor law Nigeria" occasionally
- Doesn't act because unsure of his rights

**Needs from LawMadeSimple:**
- Labour Act explained simply
- "Is this legal?" scenarios for employment situations
- What recourse exists for employees
- Understanding of at-will vs contract employment

**Quote:** *"My oga is doing me anyhow, but I don't know if it's even against the law."*

---

### Persona 4: Ngozi (The Concerned Citizen)

**Demographics:**
- Age: 42
- Occupation: Teacher
- Location: Enugu
- Tech savviness: Low-moderate (uses Facebook, WhatsApp)
- Income: ₦120,000/month

**Goals:**
- Understand her constitutional rights
- Know how to handle police encounters
- Teach her children about their rights

**Pain Points:**
- Witnessed police harassment of her nephew
- Doesn't know what police can legally do
- Constitution seems impossible to understand
- Worries about family safety

**Behaviors:**
- Watches legal advice videos on Facebook (often misleading)
- Discusses with church members
- Wants authoritative information

**Needs from LawMadeSimple:**
- Constitutional rights in plain language
- Police encounter guide ("What can they do? What can you do?")
- Printable/shareable information
- Offline access (poor internet)

**Quote:** *"I need to know my rights so I can protect my family."*
